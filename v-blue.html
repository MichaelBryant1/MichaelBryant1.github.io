BRMA Entity Review QC Framework

How BRMA reviews risk-critical files.

A structured, five-pillar QC methodology built from years of operational work in controlled, regulated review environments.

Pillar 01

CIP & Entity Identification

  • Legal entity name, EIN, and registration number verification
  • Legal structure, entity type, and jurisdiction of formation
  • DBA, trade name, and alias reconciliation against CIP records
  • Registered address vs. principal place of business (CIP reconciliation)
Pillar 02

KYB & Risk Classification

  • Customer risk classification: MSB, NBFI, PEP-adjacent, or standard
  • Business activity verification: SIC/NAICS alignment with stated NaP
  • High-risk jurisdiction review: FATF grey/black list and OFAC exposure
  • Shell company indicators, adverse media, and PEP/RCA linkage screening
Pillar 03

UBO & Ownership Structure

  • UBO identification and verification (FinCEN CDD Rule / 25% threshold)
  • GUP mapping and multi-tier ownership structure review
  • Related-party, subsidiary, and control-person review
  • PEP, RCA, and adverse media disposition with documented rationale
Pillar 04

Verification & Screening

  • CIP documentary and non-documentary verification with disposition
  • OFAC/SDN, FinCEN 314(a), and watchlist screening documentation
  • Source of Wealth (SOW) and Source of Funds (SOF) review and sufficiency
  • Nature and Purpose (NaP) statement review and risk alignment
Pillar 05

CDD File Completeness & ILQC

  • Document sufficiency review against CIP/CDD/EDD policy requirements
  • Discrepancy, red flag, and exception identification with QC notation
  • ILQC findings documentation and remediation tracking
  • Audit-ready file certification and regulatory exam preparation
